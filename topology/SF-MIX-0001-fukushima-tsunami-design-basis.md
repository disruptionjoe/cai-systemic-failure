# SF-MIX-0001 - Fukushima Tsunami Design-Basis Mixed Case

## Boundary

This mixed-case pressure test uses public official Fukushima Daiichi source
material to test whether `SF-SCHEMA-CANDIDATE-0001` can handle a case where
source-backed old-assumption reuse, design-basis inertia, nuclear and tsunami
hazard controls, severe-accident preparation, regulatory authority, and
emergency response all appear plausibly relevant.

It does not create an accepted topology schema, nuclear-safety finding,
tsunami-hazard model, regulatory conclusion, emergency-planning conclusion,
radiation-health conclusion, legal conclusion, CAI doctrine, intervention plan,
or owner routing instruction.

No confidential material, human subjects, field work, account access, external
contact, posting, spending, publication outside normal GitHub versioning, or
deployment was used.

## Mixed-Case Question

The candidate should not force a binary answer where the source set supports
both a review-chain residue and strong domain-specific absorber pressure.

The IAEA Technical Volume 2 source set supports a mixed reading. The original
Fukushima Daiichi tsunami design basis used historical tsunami records,
including the 1960 Chile tsunami level at Onahama Port, and that licensing
design basis still represented flooding generated by tsunamis at the time of
the March 2011 accident. The same source set also records later reassessment,
underestimation of extreme flooding, beyond-design-basis accident management
gaps, loss of safety functions, severe-accident preparation limits, and
regulatory or legal framework constraints.

That makes this case a useful borderline pressure test. It has apparent old
design-basis assumption reuse under changed or reassessed external-hazard
evidence. It also has strong nuclear-safety, tsunami-hazard, severe-accident,
regulatory, emergency-management, and site-protection explanations that this
repository must not flatten into generic review-chain vocabulary.

## Verdict

`SF-SCHEMA-CANDIDATE-0001` should treat Fukushima Daiichi as a mixed pressure
case, not as a sixth positive record and not as a clean negative absorber.

The candidate can represent part of the source-backed residue: an inherited
design-basis assumption remained consequential under later external-hazard
evidence and reassessment pressure, with visible operator, regulator, and
accident-management review paths. The candidate is also missing an explicit
way to name design-basis reassessment as an annotation pressure without
claiming the entire nuclear-safety, tsunami-hazard, severe-accident, regulatory,
and emergency-response explanation.

The result strengthens the candidate only if the mixed status is preserved.
Counting this case as ordinary positive evidence would overgeneralize the
candidate. Refusing it entirely would hide a plausible review-chain blind spot.

## Candidate Revision Pressure

Keep the core field set unchanged, but add one source-gated annotation pressure
to the candidate:

`design_basis_reassessment_path`: formal backchecks, reassessments, stress
tests, design-basis reviews, licensing-basis updates, or external-hazard review
paths that sources show as pending, incomplete, nonbinding, fragmented, or not
owner-visible enough.

This annotation must remain non-mandatory. It cannot let a record qualify
without the core source, old-assumption, changed-condition, validation-burden,
affected-standing, absorber, and correction-route gates. It also cannot
authorize nuclear-safety, tsunami-hazard, severe-accident-management,
emergency-planning, regulatory, radiation-health, legal, or domain-remedy
conclusions.
