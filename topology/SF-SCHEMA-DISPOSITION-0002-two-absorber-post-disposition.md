This disposition compares `SF-ABS-0002` and `SF-ABS-0003` after both negative
absorbers refused `SF-SCHEMA-CANDIDATE-0001` as positive evidence. It uses only
the repository's existing source-backed records, topology tests, schema
candidate, and public official source pointers.

systemic-failure ontology, issue a domain remedy, authorize an intervention,

## Disposition

Keep `SF-SCHEMA-CANDIDATE-0001` as a local provisional record screen. Do not
accept it as schema.

The two negative absorbers materially strengthen refusal discipline because
they reject different kinds of source-backed systemic failure:

- `SF-ABS-0002` rejects an adversarial compliance and control-bypass case;
- `SF-ABS-0003` rejects a domain-code, protection-system, and fire/life-safety
  case; and
- both refusals preserve the same core gate: positive fit requires
  source-backed assumption reuse under changed conditions, visible validation
  burden, affected standing, absorber control, and an owner-visible correction
  route.

That is enough to continue using the candidate as a disciplined local screen.
It is not enough to convert the candidate into accepted topology, CAI doctrine,
a universal vocabulary, or a recommendation surface. Negative absorbers show
that the candidate can refuse some false positives; they do not prove that the
candidate is complete, portable, or safe to promote.

## Option Decision

| Add new candidate fields now | Refuse | The current field list and source-gated annotations already preserve the observed pressures. |
| Keep as a local provisional screen | Select | This preserves record-screening value while keeping source, absorber, correction-route, and no-remedy gates active. |
| Require an acceptance-bar review before any future promotion | Select | The next step should name future promotion prerequisites and remaining falsifier classes, not accept the schema. |

## Controls Preserved

Future use of the candidate must continue to refuse cases that lack:

1. public, licensed, safely shareable, or synthetic source material;
2. a source-context-tied assumption;
3. a changed condition or changed evidence context;
4. a source-backed validation burden;
5. affected system or affected-party standing;
6. material delegation, review, request, or visibility fragmentation;
7. a narrower absorber or countermodel; and
8. an owner-visible correction route that does not require this repository to
   act as the domain owner.

The two refusal notes in `SF-SCHEMA-CANDIDATE-0001` remain active. Deliberate
control bypass, test circumvention, false reporting, domain-code application,
fire/life-safety protection, material flammability, suppression, pyrotechnic,
evacuation, inspection, emergency-response, or similar domain-specific remedy
paths should be recorded as absorbers or separate topology questions unless
sources also satisfy the candidate's assumption-reuse gates.

## Candidate Maintenance

Do not change the candidate field list now. The current source-gated
annotations remain annotations:

- `hazard_history`;
- `observation_request_path`;
- `jurisdictional_authority_gap`;
- `resident_warning_route`.

Promote an annotation only if a later source-backed record or absorber shows
that the existing fields cannot preserve the contradiction without it.

## Next Work

Before any schema acceptance, run a schema acceptance-bar review that names:

- what evidence would be required for any future promotion;
- what remaining false-positive or false-negative classes have not been tested;
- what counterevidence would kill or demote the candidate;
- whether another distinct falsifier is required before promotion can even be
  considered; and
- which owner, if any, would have authority to accept a schema.

That review should not accept the schema. It should either keep the candidate
provisional with explicit remaining tests or route an acceptance question to
the proper authority with no automatic promotion.
