# SF-0005 - Grenfell Tower Refurbishment Resident-Warning Review Chain

## Boundary

This record is a bounded public-source synthesis of the Grenfell Tower fire
source set. It tests `SF-SCHEMA-CANDIDATE-0001` in a high-rise residential
building, fire-safety, refurbishment, and resident-warning context.

It is not a building-safety engineering finding, fire-service finding,
regulatory finding, product-safety finding, civil-rights claim, legal claim,
public recommendation, campaign, remediation plan, or intervention pathway.

No confidential material, human subjects, field work, account access, external

## Pattern Summary

The bounded pattern is not "high-rise buildings fail" or "one building-safety
remedy would have prevented the disaster." The useful topology question is how
a refurbishment, external-wall, product-assurance, building-control, emergency-
planning, and resident-warning review chain could leave old safety assumptions
standing after the source context had changed.

For this repository, the record is narrow:

- the 2016 refurbishment changed the relevant evidence context by adding an
  external cladding system, altered window details, and related design
  dependencies;
- assumptions about regulatory guidance, product suitability, fire-safety
  strategy, compartmentation, and high-rise response needed accountable
  revalidation under the changed external-wall and resident-risk context;
- source-reported warning routes included prior incident learning,
  professional or coroner concerns, building-control and design review, fire
  service preparedness, and residents' reported safety concerns;
- the relevant review path crossed owner, manager, architect, contractor,
  subcontractor, fire engineer, building control, product assurance, fire
  service, and central-government guidance boundaries; and
- affected-party standing is central because residents, visitors, responders,
  nearby households, and the wider local community carried the risk while
  safety evidence and correction ownership were fragmented.

## New Correction-Route Pressure

`SF-0005` adds one pressure to keep source-gated rather than mandatory:

- `resident_warning_route`: whether public official sources show resident,
  tenant, local-community, or affected-party warnings that could have become an
  accountable review, escalation, stop, or correction route.

This pressure belongs under `affected_system_and_standing`,
`observation_environment`, `delegation_visibility_gap`, or
`correction_route_stop_condition` unless a later source-backed record or
absorber shows that those fields cannot preserve it.

The annotation must not authorize this repository to contact residents,
interpret testimony beyond public official records, run field research, create
a campaign, assign legal responsibility, or prescribe a building-safety remedy.

## Counterevidence And Absorbers

The strongest absorbers are domain-specific:

- the disaster is a building-material, facade-system, certification, or product-
  assurance case whose remedies belong to building-safety authorities and domain
  experts;
- the disaster is a fire-service preparation, evacuation, or emergency-response
  case;
- the disaster is a regulatory-guidance and enforcement case;
- the disaster is a project-management, procurement, construction, or building-
  control competence case; or
- resident and local-community warnings are primarily a governance or trust
  failure rather than a reusable review-chain field.

Those absorbers are partly right and narrow this repository's claims. They do
not absorb the candidate because this record uses them only as controls around
the source-backed contradiction: a changed building and evidence context,
unresolved validation burden, distributed review, affected standing, and
owner-visible correction routes.

## Uncertainty And Falsifiers

This synthesis may compress distinct design, construction, product-assurance,
building-control, fire-service, government-guidance, and resident-warning paths
into one review-chain pattern. It does not reconstruct individual knowledge or
settle building-safety, legal, regulatory, or emergency-response conclusions.

Falsify or narrow the record if closer source review shows that changed-context
safety assumptions, resident warnings, distributed review, and correction
ownership were already preserved as one accountable contradiction object, or
that the pattern requires a building-specific remedy to remain coherent.
